About this page

Hi there! I'm something of a collector of these 80s action figures and one day I had an idea to draw a retro pixel art image of one. A small experiment in simplification: how to present a figure in 32x32 pixels and include all the colors used in the original figure. Then I did another, and eventually all the rest of the series. Dozens of others somewhere have probably drawn similar pixel versions (because there's only so many ways to place pixels in 32x32 grid!) but I couldn't find one while searching. If you have, I assure you I was not copy-pasting. And I know that nowadays also AI could probably draw these kind of images instantly but I'm a dingus and wanted to do them by hand.

Interactivity: you can play with the sort and recolor options if you like: move mouse on a character and click color swatches to recolor single character. At the end of the page there's also an option to recolor all at once.

The other gimmick is to hold mousebutton on a character to reveal its weapon, but currently the actual images are missing.

Bonus thingy: grid animation

What are Battle Beasts anyway?

Battle Beasts were a line of small armored animal action figures produced by Takara in Japan and distributed by Hasbro in the late 1980s. Each figure stood roughly 5 cm tall and came packaged with a weapon and a heat-sensitive rubsign on its chest revealing one of three elemental symbols: Fire, Water, or Wood. Later figures in the line replaced the rub sign with a translucent orb and were known as Laser Beasts (they had laser guns instead of melee weapons). A total of 112 figures were released.
